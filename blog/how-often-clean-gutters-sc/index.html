How Often Should You Clean Your Gutters in South Carolina?

The answer depends on your trees — here's a simple guide for SC homeowners.

Quick Answer

Most South Carolina homes need gutters cleaned twice a year — once in late fall after leaves drop, and once in spring after pollen season. Homes under heavy pine or mixed tree cover may need three or four cleanouts annually.

Why South Carolina Is Harder on Gutters Than Most States

SC gets over 46 inches of rain per year, often in intense sudden bursts. That rain has to go somewhere — and if your gutters are clogged when a 3-inch storm hits, the water overflows against your fascia, saturates the soil around your foundation, and can find its way into your crawl space or basement.

On top of the rainfall, SC has year-round debris load from longleaf and loblolly pines (needles fall in every season), heavy oak cover in established neighborhoods, and pollen season that can pack fine organic material into downspouts. It adds up faster than homeowners expect.

How to Tell What Your Home Needs

No trees within 30 feet of the roofline

Once a year is likely sufficient — ideally in late fall or early winter before the heaviest rains.

Deciduous trees only (oak, maple, sweetgum)

Twice a year: late fall after leaf drop, and spring after pollen. These trees shed all at once, so the timing is predictable.

Pine trees or mixed pine and hardwood

Three to four times a year. Pines shed year-round, and needle buildup can clog a gutter in a single season. Fall, late winter, and midsummer are the critical windows.

Heavy canopy directly over the roofline

Every two to three months during active shedding periods. If you're in this situation, it's worth a conversation about gutter covers — the math changes when cleanouts happen six times a year.

Signs You're Overdue for a Cleanout

  • Water overflowing the front or back of the gutter during rain
  • Visible plant growth (moss, small seedlings) inside the gutter
  • Staining streaks on your siding below the gutter line
  • Sagging in sections of gutter (debris weight pulls hangers)
  • Water pooling at your foundation after rain

If you're seeing any of these, schedule a cleanout now. If you're tired of the recurring cost, ask us about gutter covers — the right system can cut your cleanout frequency to once every few years, or eliminate it entirely.
