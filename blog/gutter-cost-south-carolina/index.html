How Much Do Gutters Cost in South Carolina?

Updated July 2026 — real price ranges, what drives them up or down, and what to watch out for when getting quotes.

Quick Answer

Seamless aluminum gutters in South Carolina typically cost $6–$12 per linear foot installed. For an average single-story home with 150–200 linear feet of gutter, expect a total of $900–$2,400 depending on materials, roof complexity, and whether old gutters need removal.

Cost Per Linear Foot by Material

MaterialInstalled Cost / Linear FootNotes
Aluminum (seamless)$6–$12Most common in SC. Lightweight, rust-resistant, holds paint well.
Steel$8–$20Heavier and stronger than aluminum. Better for large commercial rooflines.
Copper$30–$50Premium option. Develops natural patina over time. Lasts 50+ years.

What Drives the Price Up or Down

Several factors move your final number significantly:

  • Home stories. Two-story homes require taller ladders and more setup time — expect to add 20–30% over single-story pricing.
  • Roofline complexity. Simple box shapes are fast to run. Homes with lots of corners, valleys, and dormers take longer and use more material.
  • Old gutter removal. If existing gutters need to come down before new ones go up, add $1–$2 per linear foot for removal and disposal.
  • Downspout count and length. More downspouts mean better drainage — and a slightly higher material cost. Getting this right matters more than saving $50.
  • Gutter guards. Adding covers at install time is cheaper than coming back for them later. Micro-mesh systems run $8–$15 per linear foot installed on top of gutter cost.

Seamless vs. Sectional: Why It Matters in South Carolina

Sectional gutters come in pre-cut pieces joined at seams. Every seam is a potential leak point — and in South Carolina's heat and humidity, seals degrade faster than in drier climates. A sectional gutter that installs fine in October may be dripping by the following summer.

Seamless gutters are fabricated on-site as a single continuous run with no mid-section joints. The only seams are at corners and downspout connections — and those are sealed properly.
