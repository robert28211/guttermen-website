What Happens When Gutters Stay Clogged

Ignoring clogged gutters rarely ends with just clogged gutters.

🏚️

Water Damage

When gutters overflow, water runs down your siding, behind your fascia boards, and into your foundation. The repair costs dwarf the cost of a cleanout.

🐜

Pest Infestations

Standing water and decomposing debris are ideal nesting material for mosquitoes, wasps, birds, and rodents. A clogged gutter is an open invitation.

🏗️

Foundation Issues

Gutters are designed to move water away from your foundation. When they're blocked, water pools at the base of your home — causing cracks, settling, and costly structural repairs.

🌿

Roof Damage

Debris backed up against your roof edge traps moisture and accelerates rot on the fascia, decking, and shingles — shortening your roof's lifespan significantly.

⚖️

Gutter Damage

Wet debris is heavy. Over time, the weight of a packed gutter causes hangers to loosen, sections to sag, and seams to separate — turning a cleanout into a repair job.

🌧️

Basement Flooding

In heavy rain, an overflow from clogged gutters can saturate the soil against your foundation and find its way into your basement or crawl space.

Why It Matters

When Should You Clean Your Gutters?

Most SC homes need a cleanout at least twice a year — more if you have heavy tree cover.

1

Spring

After winter, gutters are full of debris from fall and winter storms. Clean them before spring rains hit to prevent overflow and water damage.

2

Fall

After leaves have fully dropped — late November is ideal in South Carolina. Heading into winter with clean gutters prevents ice buildup and keeps drainage working through the wet season.

3

After Major Storms

A big storm can pack a season's worth of debris into your gutters overnight. If you've had a significant weather event, it's worth a check.

Common Questions

What's the best time of year to clean your gutters?

Spring and fall, before heavy rains. This prevents clogging, leaks, and water damage to your home. If you have pine trees nearby, you may need cleanouts more frequently — pine needles are small enough to slip past most debris screens and pack down quickly.

How long does a gutter cleanout take?

Most residential cleanouts take 1–2 hours depending on the size of the home and how backed up the gutters are. We clear all debris, flush the downspouts, and leave your gutters flowing freely.

Should I get gutter covers after a cleanout?

If you're tired of paying for cleanouts every season, gutter covers can significantly reduce how often gutters need attention. We install several cover systems and can recommend the right one for your roof type and tree situation. Ask us during your cleanout visit.
