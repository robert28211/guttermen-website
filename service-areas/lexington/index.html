Lexington's Growth Means More Homes That Need Proper Gutters

Lexington County has grown faster than almost any other part of South Carolina over the past two decades. That growth means a mix of newer subdivisions with large rooflines and tightly spaced homes, established neighborhoods with mature oak and pine tree cover, and lakefront properties along Lake Murray where water management is critical.

Newer construction often comes with undersized or improperly pitched gutters — builders install to minimum spec, not to what your roof actually needs. Older homes have gutters that have shifted over years of heat and humidity. The Guttermen assess what's actually happening at your roofline and fix it right — installation, repair, and maintenance that holds up in the Midlands climate.

Frequently Asked Questions — Lexington Area

Do you serve Lexington and the Lake Murray area?

Yes. Our West Columbia office is just minutes from Lexington, and we serve the entire area including Lake Murray, Batesburg-Leesville, Pelion, and Gilbert. Most Lexington jobs are on our schedule within a week.

How often should Lexington homeowners clean their gutters?

Twice a year is the standard — spring and fall. Lexington's mix of mature oaks and pines means gutters fill up faster than you'd expect. Homes on wooded lots near Lake Murray often need a third cleanout in late summer when pine needles are heaviest.

Are your gutters compatible with Lexington HOA requirements?

Almost always, yes. Most Lexington HOAs require gutters to match the home's trim color and prohibit visible fasteners — both of which our seamless aluminum gutters satisfy by default. If your HOA has specific guidelines, bring them to the quote visit and we'll confirm compliance before we start.

Do lakefront homes near Lake Murray need special gutters?

Lakefront homes benefit from larger gutters and more frequent downspout placement because steep, open rooflines tend to move water fast during heavy rain. We account for roof pitch and lot drainage when we size your system — lakefront installs are not one-size-fits-all.
