The Pee Dee's Climate Demands More from Your Gutters

Florence sits in one of South Carolina's wettest corridors, regularly experiencing heavy summer thunderstorms and tropical moisture from the coast. The combination of intense rainfall, persistent humidity, and the region's abundant pine and hardwood trees creates an environment that clogs gutters faster than homeowners expect.

Overflowing gutters in the Pee Dee saturate the sandy soil around foundations, allowing water to pool and seep into crawl spaces and basements. Fascia boards rot quickly when they stay damp. Left unaddressed, a failing gutter system can lead to mold, structural damage, and costly foundation repairs. Regular maintenance and properly installed gutters are the first line of defense.

Frequently Asked Questions — Florence Area

Do you serve the Pee Dee region?

Yes. Our Florence office serves the entire Pee Dee region including surrounding communities in Florence County and neighboring areas. Call us at (843) 662-6218 to confirm coverage for your specific location.

How often do Florence homes need gutter cleanouts?

Most Florence-area homes benefit from two cleanouts per year — once in late fall after leaf drop and again in spring. Homes near pine stands may need additional cleanouts, as pine needles pack tightly and can clog gutters and downspouts quickly during heavy rains.

Do you handle storm damage repairs in Florence?

Absolutely. The Pee Dee sees its share of severe weather — high winds, heavy rain, and the occasional tropical storm. We respond quickly to storm damage calls, assess what can be repaired versus what needs replacement, and get your system back in shape before the next storm hits.

Are seamless gutters worth it in Florence?

Yes — especially here. Sectional gutters have joints every 10 feet or so, and those joints are the most common point of failure. In Florence's humid climate, sealant at those joints breaks down faster. Seamless gutters have no mid-run joints, which means fewer leaks and less maintenance over the life of the system.
